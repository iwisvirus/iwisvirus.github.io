**Sherlock Scenario**

In this very easy Sherlock, you will familiarize yourself with Unix auth.log and wtmp logs. We'll explore a scenario where a Confluence server was brute-forced via its SSH service. After gaining access to the server, the attacker performed additional activities, which we can track using auth.log. Although auth.log is primarily used for brute-force analysis, we will delve into the full potential of this artifact in our investigation, including aspects of privilege escalation, persistence, and even some visibility into command execution.

**Task 1. Analyze the auth.log. What is the IP address used by the attacker to carry out a brute force attack?**
First this task, I took a peak at the authorization log. At first I thought the IP address is the "ip-172-31...". And then I realized that's just the internal computer's IP address not the attacker's. It literally states on the log "invalid user admin from "ip address".
